Critical Summary Below

In this reflection, I will explore the idea of game selfies, aka taking a screenshot while playing a computer game. Furthermore I will delve into why some people do take pictures of themselves playing games. Generally in social media, depending on your friends, one seldom sees a screenshot from a game. Be it solataire or Super Mario, we have all played a computer game some point of our lives, but why is it that we rare come across it on our facebook or instagram? I have come to learn that posting screenshots from your games on social media is a social faux paus. Inside the social world, computer games are considered antisocial and "nerdy". Most people in order to portray themselves in the best light as possible to their peers would probably steer clear of posting any game related material. Furthermore, in game pictures lack a sense of uniqueness that real life pictures do. Suppose you take a picture of yourself playing blackjack online, chances are there are tens of thousands of other pictures extremely similar to yours on the internet. People have already seen stuff like this and thus are disinterested. Whereas if you post a picture of yourself with a celebrity, there is probably no other picture out in the web like it. There are however, some rare cases where people do post "game selfies". People sometimes post game selfies because they are socially ignorant, or have a large group of friends who are gamers themselves. When people do post these selfies, often it is to share a certain achievement or notify people of their purchase of the game. This is because these two reason help allow a factor of uniqueness in the picture.
