From the library I checked the book called FBI most wanted by Michael & Judy Newton. The book is filled with cases of various different wanted fugitives and villains. The book is structured in entries of each fugitive with their background story along with it. To be honest ever since I started going to college I found that I read fewer and fewer books on my own accord. On a whole since middle school, I have noticed that I have been less inclined to read books on my own spare time. As a result my trips to the library have subsequently decreased. The books that I have read on my own accord are most books on science and it's theories. I think the cause for this decrease in book reading is the change in what people do during their free time now. For instance, back maybe 20 years ago it was common for people to read while on the public transportation. Now with the advent of smartphones, leisurely reading is nor starting to become a relic of the past. Even with hard copy replacements such as the kindle, when it comes to watching a tv show versus reading a book most of the time the majority of the current population would pick watching the show. I do remember what it is like to be drawn into a book series, but I feel that the initial few steps are generally the hardest when it comes to reading a book. As a result, many people might not be willing to take those steps.
