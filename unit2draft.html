posted 7:31 PM Wednesday, March 9th, 2016 (EST)

Look at the person on your right and the one on your left. Chances are that one of them is an introvert. Roughly 50% of our entire population is introverted. That means theres a good chance you are too. Don’t worry. Introversion doesn’t mean the stereotypical antisocial and socially awkward definition . According to Carl Jung, a forerunner in modern psychiatry, defines introversion as “characterized an inward flowing of personal energy - a withdrawal concentrating on subjective factors.” In simpler terms, being introverted means that someone who feels energized by spending time alone. Extroverts on the other hand feel energized by spending time with others. This means that after spending long periods of time with other people, it is typical for introverts to need time to themselves to recharge. Contrary to the popular stigma, introverts are not necessarily shy and most lead great social lives. More characteristic traits of introverts are having a rich imagination, and being reflective and introspective.
With the recent proliferation of social media, we no longer have to meet face to face with our friends. Be it Facebook, Instagram, or Snapchat, Almost everyone we know uses at least one form of social media. Given an introspective’s tendency to require time spent alone, one wonder about the relationship between introverts and social media. The general consensus might be that because introverts need to spend more time by themselves they will be less active on their respective social media platforms. However, that simply isn’t the case. I believe that introverts, contrary to popular opinion, are more frequents users of social media than their extroverted counterpart.
I conducted a survey recently to try to establish the connection between introversion and social media. In my survey, I asked my participants three questions. First, I asked them to rate how extroverted they are from 1-10. Secondly, I asked them to rate how often they check their social media from 1- 10. Lastly I asked my participants to estimate number of social media platform you use. From the results of my survey I found that there is no general correlation between introversion and the number of social media platforms. However, astonishingly, I found that the more introverted an individual is the more often they check their social media. I found this result surprising in that it goes against the common consensus. There are however a multitude of theories that can go to explaining why this is the case.
In his article “How Technology Empowers the Introverted,” Author Shane Stewart states one possible explanation which he dubs “24/7 communication”. He goes on to explain that because introverts normally feel drained after regular social contact, social media provides a convenient alternative. Because social media platforms do not require face to face confrontations, introverts can now construct well thought out responses without having the pressure of coming up with one on the spot. Furthermore because social media is available 24/7, introverts now have the option to space out their social sessions into more manageable periods. Previously, when social interaction was solely face to face the options for when to socialize was extremely limited, especially for introverts. This is because face to face socialization generally last for long extended periods of time. Now since social media is constantly on, introverts can simply chose to socialize when they are comfortable rather than when they are obligated to like in face to face confrontations
